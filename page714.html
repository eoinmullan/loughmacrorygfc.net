8 November 2015

LOUGHMACRORY 0 – 07          0 – 12 GORTIN


Seniors stumble at first playoff hurdle


The seniors still have a battle on their hands to hold on to their Division 2 status after falling to Gortin by 5 points in a divisional playoff in Greencastle today.

They will now need two straight wins or it will be junior football in 2016.

Gortin were out of the blocks much quicker and had three points on the board before Aaron Kelly opened Loughmacrory’ account with a point in the sixteenth minute. Gortin kept in control adding a further 2 points before the St Teresa’s had Niall Donaghy sent off. The sending off didn’t seem to to boost the lough men for a time as points from Nathan Kelly Mark McCullagh and Damien McAleer helped to keep them in touch at half time with Gortin still leading on a 0 – 04 to 0 – 07 scoreline.

As in the opening half it was left to Aaron Kelly to open the scoring for Loughmacrory but by that time the Glens man had extended their tally to 9. The Glens men ran out comfortable winners sending over the final four points of the contest in reply to a single point from Loughmacrory.  It was a disappointing result for the Lough lads but with a bit of renewed effort the can still retain their status which will require wins over Rock and Owen Roes.
