Saturday 5 November 2016

LOUGHMACRORY 0 -09    0 -14 MOORTOWN


Reserves bow out of championship


Having already captured the league trophy the reserve side bowed out of the championship in a competitive if somewhat ratty affair at home to Moortown on Saturday. The teams were tied at nine points apiece at full time but the travelling St Malachy's proved the stronger side in the extra time.

Loughmacrory had the better of the first half exchanges with Ciarán Ward especially putting a mighty fine display at the heart of the Loughmacrory attack. The home side took a two points lead into the break at 0 -07 to 0 -05 but the visitors came out all guns blazing for the second half and, but for good defending by full back Joey Kelly and a fine block from Keeper Brian Donaghy they might have been well on their way five minutes into the second half. Moortown had pulled ahead at 0 -08 to 0 -07 after ten minutes when a mini melee saw Mark Devlin red carded and Niall McGaughey injured and this double blow hit the St Teresa’s hard. Loughmacrory did manage to level the match at 9 each and kept in touch to leave the full time score nine points each.


 Moortown came out strongly in the first moiety of extra time and lead by three points at 0 -09 to 0 -12 at the break. Enda Ward pulled one back for the home lads but the St Malachy’s kept ahead adding a further two points in reply to a converted free from Enda Ward to finish three ahead.
