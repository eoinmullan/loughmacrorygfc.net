Fri 4 Sept 2015 Healey Park championship Q/F

LOUGHMACRORY 0 – 10      2 – 10 URNEY


Loughmacrory began well as full forward Niall Grimes opened the scoring before Urney levelled. From there Loughmacrory took control with Grimes leading the way. He took a super catch from a long ball in turned and made space to fire over. Intricate link play led to the Lough’s next score, with midfielder Daryl Kelly on hand to finish a flowing move with a well taken  point to make it 0-3 to 0-01.

Urney’s Ryan Henry pulled one back before Grimes and Kelly combined for Kelly to slot over form the edge of the D. Henry hit his second point and on 26 minutes Johnny Lafferty levelled the game at 0-3 each. Pearse Gallagher then played a superb ball out to Aaron Kelly whose great run was brought to an abrupt end and Cathal Donaghy pointed the resultant free. In first half injury time Urney’s had made it 0-06 each.

five minutes into the second half Urney was reduced to fourteen men and Loughmacrory kept pushing forward their scoring chances kept going astray. Urney were more productive at the other end hitting the games first goal four minutes later.

Lough captain Mark McCullagh perhaps should have been awarded  a free for a push in the back when inside his own square, but none was forthcoming and Stephen Lafferty was on hand to stab the ball to the net to make it 1-06 to 0 -06. Johnny Lafferty and Cathal Donaghy swapped scores before Loughmacrory’s Enda Ward receiving a straight red card. By the three quarter stage Urney were five ahead at 1-09 to 0 -07.

Johnny Lafferty added another point before substitute Ciarán Ward was fouled and Ronan Ward pointed from the free. Another pointed free from Cathal Donaghy after Aaron Kelly was pulled sown brought Loughmacrory total to nine  points but then Urney struck for their second goal to make it 2 -10 to 0 -09.

Good save from Niall Grimes made a last ditch effort to turn the game but his first shot at goal was well saved by Urney keeper and in injury time he had another go shot for goal only to see his shot fly over the crossbar.
