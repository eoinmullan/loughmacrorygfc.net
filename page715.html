Sunday 22 Nov 2015

LOUGHMACRORY 2 – 101      1– 11 OWEN ROES


Intermediate status secured for 2016

Loughmacrory sealed their place in Intermediate football for the coming year this evening in Ballinamullin with a well deserved victory over Junior challengers Owen Roes.

Loughmacrory looked the better team in the opening half and had gone ahead 1 – 02 to 0 – 00 after nine minutes thanks to a goal and point from veteran Ronan Ward either side of a Niall Grimes point. Ward’s goal came at the end of a fine Loughmacrory move up the field and Mark McCullagh gave the final pass to Ward who rattled the Roes net from 20 meters with a rasping shot. The junior side were not to be walked over however and had pulled the deficit back to 2 after ten minutes. The Loch lads took control of affairs again from there to the short whistle outscoring their opponents by 1 - 4 to 0 - 02 in the second quarter.

An excellent catch, run and score from Cathal Donaghy set the loch men on their way and two minutes later his namesake Benny Donaghy split the posts. Niall Grimes then showed some brilliant skills as he took the Roes defence to the cleaners scoring a goal and two points  within six minutes to leave  his side comfortably ahead 2 – 06 to 0 – 05 at half time.

The St Teresa’s opened the second half well and had extended their lead to 8 points in the first minute before the North Tyrone men hit a purple patch  sending over four points in reply to a single score form the division 2 side. The game really opened up ten minutes into the half when The Roes were awarded a goal. Loughmacrory keeper Ryan Keenan had the ball in his hands but was tackled on his own goal line and the ball rolled over the line.                   …continued
